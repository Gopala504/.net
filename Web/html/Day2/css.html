Radha Krishna

Radha Krishna is a popular Hindu deity worshipped by millions of people around the world. They are often depicted together in art and literature, symbolizing the divine love between them. Radha is considered the supreme goddess and the beloved of Lord Krishna, who is regarded as the supreme god in Hinduism. Their love story is celebrated in various festivals and rituals, and they are revered for their devotion and spiritual significance.

Radha Krishna's story is often associated with the Bhakti movement, which emphasizes devotion and love for God. Their relationship is seen as a metaphor for the soul's longing for union with the divine. Devotees of Radha Krishna often engage in devotional practices such as singing bhajans (devotional songs), performing rituals, and participating in festivals like Janmashtami (the birth of Lord Krishna) and Radhashtami (the birth of Radha).
